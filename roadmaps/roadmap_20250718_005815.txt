**Data Science Roadmap: A Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Started**

**Step 1: Foundations (Estimated Time: 2-3 weeks)**

* **Learning Objectives:**
	+ Understand the basics of data science
	+ Familiarize yourself with programming languages and tools
	+ Learn to work with data
* **Recommended Resources:**
	+ Online courses:
		- Codecademy's Introduction to Python
		- Coursera's Data Science Specialization (Weeks 1-3)
	+ Books:
		- "Python Crash Course" by Eric Matthes
		- "Data Science for Dummies" by Lillian Pierson
* **Action Plan:**
	1. Complete Codecademy's Introduction to Python course
	2. Watch the first three weeks of Coursera's Data Science Specialization
	3. Read "Python Crash Course" and "Data Science for Dummies"

**Step 2: Programming Fundamentals (Estimated Time: 4-6 weeks)**

* **Learning Objectives:**
	+ Master Python programming
	+ Learn data structures and algorithms
	+ Practice coding with exercises and projects
* **Recommended Resources:**
	+ Online courses:
		- Python.org's official tutorials
		- Coursera's Python for Everybody (Specialization)
	+ Books:
		- "Automate the Boring Stuff with Python" by Al Sweigart
		- "Python for Data Analysis" by Wes McKinney
* **Action Plan:**
	1. Complete Python.org's official tutorials
	2. Finish Coursera's Python for Everybody specialization
	3. Read "Automate the Boring Stuff with Python" and "Python for Data Analysis"
	4. Practice coding with exercises and projects from online resources (e.g., LeetCode, Project Euler)

**Step 3: Data Science Fundamentals (Estimated Time: 4-6 weeks)**

* **Learning Objectives:**
	+ Learn data preprocessing and cleaning
	+ Understand data visualization and statistical analysis
	+ Familiarize yourself with popular data science libraries
* **Recommended Resources:**
	+ Online courses:
		- Coursera's Data Science Specialization (Weeks 4-6)
		- edX's Data Science Essentials
	+ Books:
		- "Data Preprocessing" by Dr. Dheeru Dua
		- "Visualize This: The FlowingData Guide to Design, Visualization, and Statistics" by Nathan Yau
* **Action Plan:**
	1. Complete the remaining weeks of Coursera's Data Science Specialization
	2. Watch edX's Data Science Essentials course
	3. Read "Data Preprocessing" and "Visualize This"
	4. Practice data preprocessing and visualization with exercises and projects

**Step 4: Specialization (Estimated Time: 8-12 weeks)**

* **Learning Objectives:**
	+ Choose a specialization area (e.g., machine learning, natural language processing, data visualization)
	+ Learn advanced techniques and tools specific to your chosen area
	+ Develop a portfolio of projects and applications
* **Recommended Resources:**
	+ Online courses:
		- Coursera's Machine Learning Specialization
		- edX's Natural Language Processing with Deep Learning
		- DataCamp's Data Visualization with Python
	+ Books:
		- "Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Christopher Bishop
		- "Natural Language Processing (almost) from Scratch" by Collobert et al.
		- "Data Visualization: A Handbook for Data Driven Design" by Andy Kirk
* **Action Plan:**
	1. Choose a specialization area and relevant courses
	2. Complete the chosen courses and read relevant books
	3. Develop a portfolio of projects and applications in your chosen area
	4. Practice and apply your skills to real-world problems

**Step 5: Advanced Learning (Estimated Time: 4-6 weeks)**

* **Learning Objectives:**
	+ Learn advanced techniques and tools in your chosen specialization area
	+ Stay up-to-date with the latest developments and trends in data science
	+ Network with professionals and peers in the field
* **Recommended Resources:**
	+ Online courses:
		- Coursera's Advanced Machine Learning Specialization
		- edX's Advanced Natural Language Processing
		- DataCamp's Advanced Data Visualization with Python
	+ Conferences and meetups:
		- Attend data science conferences and meetups
		- Participate in online forums and discussions
* **Action Plan:**
	1. Complete advanced courses in your chosen specialization area
	2. Attend conferences and meetups
	3. Participate in online forums and discussions
	4. Read research papers and stay up-to-date with the latest developments in data science

**Step 6: Capstone Project (Estimated Time: 8-12 weeks)**

* **Learning Objectives:**
	+ Apply your skills and knowledge to a real-world project
	+ Develop a comprehensive understanding of data science concepts and techniques
	+ Showcase your skills to potential employers and peers
* **Recommended Resources:**
	+ Online courses:
		- Coursera's Capstone Project in Data Science
		- edX's Data Science Capstone
	+ Books:
		- "Data Science for Business" by Foster Provost and Tom Fawcett
		- "Visualize This: The FlowingData Guide to Design, Visualization, and Statistics" by Nathan Yau
* **Action Plan:**
	1. Choose a capstone project idea and scope
	2. Complete the capstone project and write a report
	3. Showcase your project on platforms like Kaggle or GitHub
	4. Share your project with potential employers and peers

**Timeline:**

* Step 1: 2-3 weeks
* Step 2: 4-6 weeks
* Step 3: 4-6 weeks
* Step 4: 8-12 weeks
* Step 5: 4-6 weeks
* Step 6: 8-12 weeks

**Total Estimated Time:** 30-45 weeks (approximately 6-9 months)

Remember, this roadmap is just a suggestion, and you should adjust the pace and content to fit your learning style and goals. Good luck on your data science journey!
